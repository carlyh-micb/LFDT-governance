# Trust over IP Foundation (ToIP) Q1 Report – 2026

## Overview

During Q1 2026, the Trust over IP Foundation (ToIP) continued advancing standards development, governance modernization, and ecosystem collaboration related to decentralized digital trust infrastructure.

Key focus areas included:
- specification approval processes,
- KERI ecosystem publication work,
- decentralized trust infrastructure,
- governance coordination with LF Decentralized Trust (LFDT),
- and ecosystem collaboration around AI trust and trust registries.

---

# Progress Against Goals

Key progress during Q1 included:
- continued rollout of the ToIP Approved Deliverable process;
- advancement of KERI ecosystem specifications;
- initiation of the review process for TRQP v2.0;
- expansion of decentralized trust graph initiatives;
- and continued collaboration with LFDT TAC leadership.

ToIP also finalized a revised cadence for All-Member Meetings, which will now occur twice yearly prior to IIW events.

---

# Deliverables and Outputs

Major outputs and activities included:
- KERI specification review and publication activities;
- DOI publication planning using Zenodo;
- establishment of review committees and governance workflows;
- initiation of the ToIP Approved Deliverable process for TRQP v2.0;
- 2026 Steering Committee elections and onboarding;
- participation in LFDT Member Summit activities;
- and continued work related to Verifiable Trust Infrastructure (VTI), OpenVTC, and decentralized trust graph initiatives.

The Steering Committee also reviewed progress related to the Utah SEDI initiative and broader public-sector trust infrastructure developments.

---

# Goals for the Rest of 2026

Goals for the remainder of 2026 include:
- advancing approved deliverables through publication workflows;
- improving specification templates and documentation consistency;
- increasing ecosystem adoption visibility;
- strengthening collaboration with LFDT and partner organizations;
- and continuing work related to AI trust, trust registries, and decentralized trust infrastructure.

---

# Maintainer and Contributor Diversity

ToIP continued participation from contributors across:
- standards organizations,
- industry,
- research institutions,
- consultancies,
- and open source communities.

The Steering Committee expanded to 16 members for 2026, reflecting continued contributor engagement and organizational diversity.

---

# Challenges

Current challenges include:
- scaling governance and review processes;
- improving tooling and publication consistency;
- and maintaining operational capacity across a volunteer-driven community.

---

# Requests for TAC Support

ToIP would benefit from continued TAC support related to:
- standards publication guidance including naming conventions for ToIP-approved deliverables,
- reporting frameworks for JDF projects,
- and cross-project collaboration opportunities.

---

# Lifecycle Assessment

The Trust over IP Foundation continues to demonstrate active governance, ongoing standards development, and expanding ecosystem participation across decentralized digital trust infrastructure initiatives.
